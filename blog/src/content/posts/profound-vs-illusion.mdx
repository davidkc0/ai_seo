- **Profound** is an enterprise AI visibility platform backed by $58M in funding. It offers deep analytics, content generation, and shopping visibility — with plans starting at $99/month (billed yearly) that only track ChatGPT.
- **Illusion** is a focused AI search tracker built for startups and growing SaaS companies. It monitors ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity from day one, with plans starting at $19/month.
- Both tools solve the same core problem: *does AI recommend your product?* The difference is scope, audience, and how much you'll pay to answer that question.

## Why this comparison matters

A year ago, nobody was tracking whether ChatGPT mentioned their product. Today there's a real category forming around AI visibility — and two very different philosophies for how to approach it.

Profound has raised $58 million across three rounds and is building a full-stack enterprise platform: prompt analytics, content agents, shopping visibility, partner programs, SOC 2 compliance. They're going after the Walmarts and Docusigns of the world.

Illusion takes a different approach. We believe most companies don't need a 12-feature platform to answer a simple question: *are AI models recommending my product, and if not, what do I do about it?*

This isn't a hit piece. Profound builds good software. But their product is designed for a different buyer — and their pricing reflects it. This post breaks down where each tool wins so you can decide which one fits your actual needs.

### Why the gap exists — and what it means

Profound raised $58 million in venture capital. They have enterprise sales teams, SOC 2 compliance infrastructure, partner programs, and a content generation engine. All of that costs money to build and maintain, and it gets passed through to the customer.

Illusion is a lean product built by a small team. We don't have a partner program. We don't have a sales team. We don't generate blog posts for you. What we do is monitor the four major AI models, tell you exactly where you stand, and give you a prioritized action plan — at a price point that doesn't require budget approval from your CFO.

**This isn't cheaper because it's less capable at the core job.** It's cheaper because we don't charge you for features designed for 50-person marketing departments. If you're a founder, a solo marketer, or a small growth team, you're paying enterprise overhead for capabilities you'll never touch.

Think of it this way: Profound is Jira — powerful, configurable, built for large orgs. Illusion is [Linear](https://linear.app) — faster, leaner, and designed for teams that want to ship instead of configure.

## Who Profound is built for

Give credit where it's due. Profound has built real enterprise infrastructure:

- **SOC 2 Type II compliance** — if your procurement team requires it, you need it. Illusion doesn't have this yet.
- **Content generation agents** — if you have a content team that needs to produce AI-optimized articles at scale, Profound's agent workflow is a real time-saver.
- **Shopping visibility** — if you sell physical products and need to track ChatGPT Shopping results, Profound is one of the few tools that does this.
- **Prompt volume data** — knowing *how many people* ask a given query is valuable for prioritization. Illusion doesn't offer this yet.
- **Brand customers** — Profound lists Indeed, Docusign, LG, and Walmart as customers. If you're operating at that scale with dedicated AEO teams, their platform makes sense.

If you're spending $10K+/month on SEO tooling already, Profound's pricing isn't a stretch. It's another line item in an existing budget.

## Who Illusion is built for

Illusion exists because we looked at the AI visibility market and saw a gap: every tool was priced for enterprise, but the teams that needed the data most urgently — startups, bootstrapped SaaS companies, early-stage growth teams — were priced out.

Here's what Illusion does well:

- **Four AI models from day one.** You don't need to upgrade to track Claude or Gemini. Every plan queries ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity.

## What Illusion doesn't do (yet)

Transparency matters in a comparison post. Here's what Profound offers that Illusion doesn't:

- **Prompt volume data.** Illusion doesn't tell you how many people ask a given query. You bring the queries you care about; we tell you what AI says.
- **Content generation.** Illusion doesn't write blog posts or optimize your existing content. We show you what to fix; you (or your writer) fix it.
- **Shopping visibility.** If you sell physical products through ChatGPT Shopping, Illusion doesn't track that.
- **SOC 2 compliance.** If your company requires SOC 2 from every vendor, Illusion isn't there yet.
- **Enterprise features.** No custom deployments, no on-premises options, no dedicated account managers.

If these are dealbreakers for your team, Profound is the better choice. We'd rather be honest about that than oversell.
